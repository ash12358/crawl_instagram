I’ve been planning a trip to London to see @vamuseum’s “Winnie-the-Pooh: Exploring a Classic” since seeing #GoodbyeChristopherRobin in October. Winnie-the-Pooh didn’t really do anything for me growing up, but the movie got me interested in the author and the story behind the story. Winnie was a real-life bear from Winnipeg, Manitoba, adopted into the Second Canadian Infantry by Lt. Harry Colebourn, who’s great granddaughter wrote the book, “Winnie: The true story of the bear that inspired Winnie-the-Pooh” (which I’ve read) He brought her to the London Zoo to live, and that is where real Christopher Robin met and named his teddy bear after her! The books brought happiness to people after the war, but the real Christopher Robin grew up to despise his father for portraying him as a girly child playing with stuffed toys. He separated himself from his family and wouldn’t acknowledge the books, though he was the one who asked A. A. Milne to write them as a bedtime story when he was young. The movie is dark and disturbing, really made for adults. In the movie he enlists and they allude to him dying at war.

One more day left living in "The one and only Bushwick" as Pauline used to call it (yeah she gets a shout out). Of course I've got to say, the memories here and the friends I met (and the crazy's) were all within some of the best years of my New York life, thanks to this special, timeless Brooklyn neighborhood. Anyone who should come here is lucky. You feel the same history that inspired the stories and writers who indulged the Brooklyn inclination before you; where little trees grow in the cracks of the sidewalk, the M train swerves on a rickety old track over Myrtle and the grill smoke on every block of Troutman taints your clothes & hair... everyone's a neighbor here, everyone's seen each other before on the street or danced with them at Tandem or stood in the bathroom line together at the Three Diamond Door. I'll be back every Friday night to sweat it up inside the Bogart galleries and watch random people in thongs dance outside of House of Yes with an old school boom box speaker blasting Madonna. Ugh! This is the coolest neighborhood.
