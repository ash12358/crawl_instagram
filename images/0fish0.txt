Here‚Äôs a little tutorial on how I built the 3ft tall Minotaur head. Swipe through for progress pics! Eyes were made by @fourthsealstudios ‚û°Ô∏è 1.  Sculpted a 1 ft wide #wedclay maquette to get the proportions down.  2. Made a numbered tape template keeping note of all of the edges and curves. 3. Cut the tape pieces off and scanned/traced them on the computer.  Enlarged the pieces 3x (since the original sculpt was 1ft and we want the full size to be 3ft). Used a #SilhouetteCameo cutter to make the pattern pieces on butcher paper and cut #evafoam with those pieces. 5) Assembled the rough shape for the Minotaur and carved a horn shape in pink insulation foam. 6)Glued on poly foam muscles to build out the form. 7) Stretched thin poly foam over the head for skin and glued it down with spray adhesive.  Left areas loose to create natural folds and wrinkles. Used a razor blade to trim and carve any rough edges. 8)Coated the head with balloon rubber latex to create a skin texture and smooth any rough spots. Sponge painted a mix of balloon rubber and paint to create the base.  Then airbrushed areas to brighten it up and break up the color.  Dry brush painted the horns to add texture and detailing.  9)3d modeled and printed a nose ring and aged with silver spray paint and black acrylic washes. Carved teeth out of pink foam. Added #EpsilonPro for clear snot and drool. Then it was finished! üòÖ
