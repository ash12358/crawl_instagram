Today we’re celebrating Mary Church Terrell, one of the first African American women to earn a college degree from Oberlin in 1884. In this photo from 1954, a 90-year-old Terrell reads a cover story announcing the Brown v. Board decision and the end of legal segregation in public schools. Terrell was a lifelong civil rights activist, founding the National Association of Colored Women in 1896 and eventually becoming a charter member of the NAACP. A fierce advocate for integration, she helped take down segregated restaurants in Washington, D.C. by suing a whites-only restaurant in 1950 and setting the stage for a later court order that ruled segregated restaurants in the city were unconstitutional. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . #oberlin #college #oberlincollege #ohio #midwest #midwestisbest #blackhistorymonth #bhm #civilrights #activism #activists #marychurchterrell #brownvboard #supremecourt #protest #washingtondc #herstory #blackwomenshistory #womenshistory #themoreyouknow #tbt #studentlife #campuslife #collegelife

On this day of Black History Month, we’re highlighting Mary Jane Patterson (1840-1894), the first Black woman to earn a B.A. in the United States. An 1862 graduate of Oberlin College, Patterson went on to teach at the Institute for Colored Youths and later became principal of a high school in Washington, D.C. that served Black students planning on attending college. Patterson was one of four members of her family that eventually graduated from Oberlin, where she and her family relocated in the mid 1800s. The Pattersons were among dozens of African American families who settled in Oberlin during the Civil War Era. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . #oberlin #college #oberlincollege #ohio #collegelife #campuslife #studentlife #student #life #campus #blackhistorymonth #bhm #history #ushistory #civilwar #themoreyouknow #maryjanepatterson #blackwomen #womenshistory #2018

Hey y'all! My name is Tess Joosse (she/her/hers). I'm a third year Biology major/Anthropology minor from Chicago, and this semester I'm studying abroad with the Danenberg Oberlin in London Program. This program is really unique: every spring semester about 25 Oberlin students and two Oberlin professors from different departments travel to London to take/teach courses in their own subject, as well as an interdisciplinary course all together. This semester the program is lead by Maureen Peters (Biology) and Drew Wilburn (Classics), with a focus on the history of medicine. I was drawn to the London program because sometimes it's hard for science majors to fit study abroad into a rigid course trajectory, and the curriculum this semester gave me the opportunity to take Oberlin biology classes abroad. I'm also interested in public health and healthcare reform, so when I heard about this semester's medical focus, I was sold! London and the U.K. have a rich medical history (think the invention of vaccination, Florence Nightingale, the discovery of the DNA double helix, penicillin!), and so far I've loved using the city as my classroom. Throughout the rest of the semester I'll be popping up here to highlight some of the cool things we're doing with the program -- hope you enjoy! :) #OberlinInLondon #StudentTakeover . . . . . #Oberlin #OberlinCollege #London #medicine #science #liberalarts #biology
