Birthday Reflection I didn't have anything in particular planned for my birthday. Honestly I needed to sit down somewhere because I've been on the go a bit too much in the last month. But I decided to go home (hometown home) to visit some folks that needed visiting. My uncle is very ill and my grandma is in this nursing home that I hate. My mother was talking about how her sister left this earth at 45 due to complications with dialysis because she had kidney disease. And I thought to myself "Wow, I just turned 46!" In my mind, I had misremembered thinking she was younger than that. It was her death, in fact, that actually caused me to change how I go about life. I do everything that I do because, quite simply, tomorrow is not promised. I live life with a sense of urgency. But even without some potential medical condition looming over me, the same is true - we don't know how long we will be on this earth. My end goal in this life is that (even though I hate my grandmother's nursing home) if I end up in one, I will be that old lady telling stories that no one believes but that are 100% true. I also felt like it came full circle since I decided to help raise money for the National Kidney Foundation for my birthday in addition to participating in the Kidney Walk this weekend. My aunt left this earth in her 40s and so did my grandfather, for the same reason polycystic kidney disease (PKD). Since this particular disease is genetic, there's no cure. But they're are treatment options. NKF supports those options. If you feel inclined to donate, please do so at https://1koa.me/donate .
