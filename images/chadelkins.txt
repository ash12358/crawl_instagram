Exploring the Map: Baltimore Row. Built in 1885, it is Atlanta's oldest commercial structure and one of the city's oldest landmarks. This area of Peachtree street north of the city proper had become a premiere location for residency of Atlanta's affluent citizens. Baltimore Row (also known as Baltimore Block or Place) was not only the first type of this style structure in the area, it was the first apartment building in the city. It is called Baltimore Row because it was built by real estate developers from Baltimore working for the "Atlanta Land and Annuity Company". This style of home, while quite popular up north, was unique to a growing southern city. The detail in the map shows all 14 of the original connected homes, but if you visit the historic building today - located between West Peachtree and Spring just south of North Ave - you'll notice that only 8 remain.
