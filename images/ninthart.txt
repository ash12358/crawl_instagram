#inktober 31.  And so, inktober ends and for this final entry the Ninth Art homage machine pays tribute to the OuBaPo. It would take too long to explain OuBaPo here, but basically it's comics made under a constraint, usually mathematical. In this strip we're further paying homage to one of the founding fathers of the OuBaPo movement and certainly one of its most prolific members, the amazing Etienne L√©croart. In his collection Comptes et D√©comptes, L√©croart created an oubapienne elegy to his recently deceased sister using a word-counting constraint. Consisting of 50 panels, and starting with a 50-word caption, he removed a word per panel until the last panel had only one word. Further, he removed a line from the image per panel so that the 1st panel had an image made up of 50 lines, right up to the 50th panel which had an image consisting of only one line. I've used this constraint in this 9-panel strip, with each panel containing the same number of words and elements (lines, blocks of colour etc) as its position in the grid. Nowhere near as impressive (or as beautifully made) as L√©croart's to be sure, but I hope it's a fitting offering. #inktober2016 #comics #illustration #ink #sketchbook #oubapo #etiennelecroart
