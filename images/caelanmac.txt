Drinking #coffee in New Zealand has introduced me to the benefits of Americanos. This drink was invented by Italians in the 40s for American soldiers, who thought the closest they could get to drip coffee with their temperamental espresso machines was to just do an absurdly long pull of espresso. While living in America, I never much cared for Americanos, preferring a dark roast drip coffee over anything an espresso machine could create. But here, in New Zealand, nobody uses a drip coffee machine. Espresso machines are everywhere. Sometimes you can find a French Press (they call it 'plunger' coffee) but drip coffee doesn't exist here. I drink from a French Press at home, but anytime I am out somewhere, I order an Americano, which is a double shot of espresso with added hot water. It's grown on me. Maybe it's because in the absence of drip coffee, this is the closest I can find, and the most suitable for my palate; just like when it was invented in Italy nearly a century ago, for Americans living in a far-off land.
